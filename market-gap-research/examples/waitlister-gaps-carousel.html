@parikshitpruthi 02 / 09
1
Gap 01 Use-case expansion

It's a page builder pretending to be a waitlist tool.

"I find myself wishing I could use the same page builder for promos, event RSVPs, and lead capture forms." AppSumo · NicolayLeg · 2026
Wedge
Unbundle the page builder. Same primitive, multiple jobs — like Notion did.
@parikshitpruthi 03 / 09
2
Gap 02 Customer journey

The day you launch is the day you leave.

"We're built for before launch. Once you've shipped, you'll outgrow us — at that point you want in-app referral mechanics tied to user IDs." Founder, waitlister.me · honest comparison page · 2026
Wedge
From waitlist to referral, in one tool. Convert queue position into post-launch credit.
@parikshitpruthi 04 / 09
3
Gap 03 UX / polish debt

The 4-star stuff.

"Reviewers note the dashboard requires more clicks than expected, and signup forms don't capture social handles (Twitter, LinkedIn) by default." AppSumo · summary across 13 reviews · 2026
Wedge
Modern dashboard UX + social fields default-on. Not a category, but the detail people quote when they switch.
@parikshitpruthi 05 / 09
4
Gap 04 Localization

Half-bilingual kills conversion.

"The landing page lets me write copy in any language, but the signup flow buttons, error messages, and confirmation emails are all English-locked." AppSumo · John.doe · 2026
Wedge
Full UI translations across 8–12 languages. Own LATAM, MENA, SEA before anyone tries.
@parikshitpruthi 06 / 09
5
Gap 05 Architectural wedge

Hosted-first is a tax on builders.

"Every waitlist tool I tried is hosted-page-first. I wanted to embed the queue directly into my Next.js app, control the design, and just hit an API." Reddit r/SideProject · founder of pleasehold.dev · 2026
Wedge
API-first waitlist primitive. Hosted page as optional layer. Pricing: per-API-call.
@parikshitpruthi 07 / 09
What's NOT a gap Don't waste your bet

Don't bet against these.

Pricing is competitive ($15/mo + a real free tier). Integrations are 30+ deep — Mailchimp, Resend, Slack, Zapier, Notion. The AI page builder is the actual differentiator and reviewers love it. Compete on something else.

Why this matters
A wedge built against a strength is dead on arrival. Pick gaps, not strengths.
